The Man, the Myth, the Wildly Egotistical
Chris Miehe began his life in the crumbling dystopian metropolis of Lethbridge, Alberta in the spring of 1991. His early life was arduous, and suffering was rampant due to video game graphics not yet having achieved a satisfactory number of polygons.

Few remember the horrors inflicted on the eyes in the time before polygons, where characters were represented by a scant few individual pixels, but Chris remembers. Many were adopted by retro gaming, but he was born into it, moulded by it. He did not know the glory of the PS3 until he was already a man. This left a significant impression on the fragile psyche stored by the quivering meat between his temples.

After significant therapy, Chris Miehe is now a jack-of-all-trades and aspiring creative professional. He has some degree of experience In nearly every common trade. However, having seen the physical state of old tradesmen, he is seeking a path of employment that is creatively empowering without the side effect of destroying every joint and ligament in his body.

In the last several years Chris has trained himself in 3D printing, 3D modeling and painting. He has designed and sold many unique pieces including a 183 piece fully printed settlers of catan set and a wall-sized version of Geraltâ€™s necklace from The Witcher. Since taking up these new hobbies Chris has rediscovered a sincere love of art and has developed a deep desire to create beauty in an ugly world.
